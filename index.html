10 “Silent” Signs of a Heart Attack That You Should Know

February 22, 2023 • News Center

Chest pain and shortness of breath are widely recognized heart‑attack symptoms. But some warning signs can be subtle and easier to overlook.

“It’s not chest pain, it’s pressure.”

Silent heart attack signs you might ignore

  • Chest pressure (a heavy or squeezing feeling).
  • Unusual fatigue that is out of proportion to activity.
  • Shortness of breath, especially with simple exertion.
  • Sudden sweating without clear cause.
  • Sense of doom — a sudden feeling that something is very wrong.

The goal is to recognize patterns and seek appropriate care when symptoms are concerning. Individuals can experience symptoms differently.

Why symptoms can be “silent”

Some people don’t feel classic chest pain. Others notice vague, flu‑like symptoms or just “not feeling right.” Factors like age, stress, and other medical conditions can also change how symptoms present.

Other atypical symptoms (examples)

  • Discomfort that radiates to the jaw, neck, back, or arms.
  • Nausea, indigestion, or stomach discomfort.
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness.
  • Sleep disturbance or sudden weakness.

When to call for emergency help

If symptoms are sudden, severe, or feel unusual for you, don’t try to “wait it out.” Seek emergency evaluation.

If you think you may be having a heart attack, call your local emergency number.
